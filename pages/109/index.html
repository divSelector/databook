If Raiden were doing VR training based on the Tanker mission, it would have to be a simulation about something as specific as blowing up the tanker and causing a massive oil spill. Anything else would undermine the core narrative of MGS2 and go against the motivations of the Patriots. When you play as Snake in the Tanker mission, you're seeing the truth of what actually happened. We find out that Ocelot is the one who blew up the tanker, and that the tanker was never carrying any oil, it was empty. On top of that, the ship was under the protection of the Marines, who were guarding it for the development of a new Metal Gear prototype. This is all top secret information, not public knowledge, which would make it really strange if Raiden was somehow learning about this in a VR simulation. It doesn't really align with the larger story, other than if it were another MGSception moment (you never really killed Big Boss!). Also, remember that the VR training Raiden underwent was based on the VR Missions game released between MGS1 and MGS2. That's where Raiden's VR training comes from, not the actual events of the Tanker mission. When you beat the VR Missions, you're actually shown blueprints for Metal Gear Ray, which is significant because that's Raiden's mission briefing after completing his training. So, the VR missions are really a separate entity that served to prepare Raiden for the real mission ahead; not for something as complex and hidden as the events in the Tanker mission. The Tanker mission is about Snake uncovering secrets and foiling the Marines' (and by extension the government's) attempt to quietly advance a new Metal Gear project. If it were just part of Raiden's VR training, it would completely spoil the mystery and take away from the shock of what's actually happening in the game.
